But the deeper logic: Malaysia is simultaneously an oil exporter and an energy-import-dependent industrial economy — manufacturing, transport, and aviation cost increases will pass through to consumers quickly. If the ringgit weakens against the dollar (as safe-haven flows go to USD), imported inflation will compound. Actionable insight: For Malaysian readers, the current window favors locking in gold or USD-denominated assets rather than holding large ringgit deposits.

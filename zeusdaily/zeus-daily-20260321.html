哲思REFLECTION

今天是开斋节——一个关于饥饿的节日。整整一个月，你学习饥饿，学习口渴，学习在匮乏中寻找意义。然后在今天，你终于被允许感恩。但感恩的前提是拥有。当伊朗红新月会报告1400人死亡、204名儿童再也不会睁开眼睛，当黎巴嫩100万人流离失所无家可归，"感恩"这个词本身就成了一种特权。而在马来西亚，349万辆车正在高速公路上回家，收费站打了五折，孩子们穿着新衣服等椰浆饭。同一颗星球，同一天。有人在庆祝活着，有人已无法庆祝。也许斋月教给我们的不是忍耐，而是这个真相：当拥有本身成为特权，感恩的最高形式不是祈祷——是行动。
——饥饿教会你感恩，但死亡剥夺你感恩的资格。

Today is Eid al-Fitr — a festival born from hunger. For an entire month, you learn hunger, learn thirst, learn to find meaning in deprivation. Then today, you are finally permitted to be grateful. But gratitude presupposes possession. When the Iranian Red Crescent reports 1,400 dead and 204 children who will never open their eyes again, when one million people in Lebanon are displaced with no home to return to, the word "gratitude" itself becomes a privilege. Meanwhile in Malaysia, 3.49 million cars are on the highway heading home, toll booths offer 50% discounts, children wear new clothes waiting for nasi lemak. Same planet. Same day. Some celebrate being alive. Others can no longer celebrate at all. Perhaps what Ramadan teaches us is not endurance, but this truth: when possession itself is privilege, the highest form of gratitude is not prayer — it is action.
— Hunger teaches gratitude, but death revokes the right to be grateful.
